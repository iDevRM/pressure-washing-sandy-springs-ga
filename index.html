What Is Pressure Washing in Sandy Springs?

Pressure washing uses a concentrated stream of water, ranging from 1,300 up to 3,100 PSI, to blast dirt, algae, oil, and grime off hard surfaces like concrete, brick pavers, and composite decking. It's different from soft washing, which relies on a low-pressure cleaning solution instead of raw force and is meant for more delicate surfaces like vinyl siding or roof shingles.

Homeowners around Sandy Springs typically need both services on the same property. A driveway can take full pressure, but the same PSI on painted wood trim or old brick mortar can chip the surface. Any crew working on your home should know which method belongs where, and switch equipment accordingly instead of running everything through one nozzle.

When Do You Need Pressure Washing?

  • Dark streaks running down your roofline. That's usually algae (Gloeocapsa magma), not dirt, and it spreads if left alone through a full Georgia summer.
  • Your driveway looks orange or rust-colored after rain. Red clay runoff from nearby soil settles into concrete pores and gets worse every storm.
  • Green or black film on your deck, walkway, or fence. Shaded, humid spots hold moisture longer and grow mold faster than sun-exposed areas.
  • You're getting ready to sell or list your home. A clean exterior is one of the cheapest ways to move the needle on curb appeal photos.
  • It's been more than 12 months since your last wash. Pollen season alone coats every surface in a yellow-green film every spring here.

How Pressure Washing Works in Sandy Springs

  1. Walkthrough and quote. We look at the surfaces involved (concrete, siding material, roof type) and give you a firm price before any work starts.
  2. Pre-treat with a cleaning solution. For siding, roofs, and anything with algae or mildew, we apply a solution first and let it dwell for 10 to 15 minutes so it can break down organic growth at the root.
  3. Wash at the right pressure for the surface. Concrete and composite decking get full pressure; siding, brick, and roofing get the soft wash treatment at 150 to 300 PSI.
  4. Rinse and inspect. We rinse thoroughly, then walk the property with you to point out any stains that need a second treatment (some oil and rust stains only lift 75 to 90 percent on the first pass).
  5. Cleanup. Plants near the work area get rinsed of any cleaning solution, and we sweep away loose debris before we leave.

Is Pressure Washing Right for You?

If your driveway has visible staining, your siding shows dark streaks, or it's simply been over a year since anything got cleaned, pressure washing (or soft washing, depending on the surface) is worth the call. It's a same-day fix for most single-property jobs.

It's probably not the right call if you have a cracked or heavily spalling driveway — pressure washing won't fix structural concrete damage, and blasting a already-compromised surface can make cracks worse. Same goes for very old, brittle roof shingles that are already curling or missing granules; a roofer should look at those before any washing happens. We'll tell you honestly if that's what we're looking at instead of taking the job anyway.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does pressure washing cost in Sandy Springs?

What's the difference between pressure washing and soft washing?

Pressure washing uses 1,300 to 3,100 PSI and is meant for tough surfaces like concrete driveways and composite decking. Soft washing uses only 150 to 300 PSI along with a cleaning solution, and it's the right call for vinyl or wood siding, roof shingles, brick, and gutters, since full pressure on those surfaces can peel paint or crack shingles. We pick the method based on the surface, not a one-size-fits-all setting.

How often should I have my house washed living in Georgia?

Most Sandy Springs homeowners wash their siding once a year, usually in spring after pollen season ends, since the humid climate here keeps mold and mildew growing year-round on shaded or north-facing walls. North-facing siding under tree cover sometimes needs a touch-up wash twice a year because it dries out slower after rain.

Will pressure washing remove red clay stains from my driveway?

Yes, in most cases. Red clay runoff is one of the most common driveway stains we see in this area, and hot water pressure washing lifts the vast majority of it. Stains that have set for years or worked deep into porous concrete may need a second pass or a mild acid wash, which we'll flag before starting so there are no surprises on the bill.

Is soft washing safe for my roof shingles?

Yes, that's exactly why soft washing exists. Standard pressure washing can strip the protective granules off asphalt shingles and void your roofing warranty. Soft washing uses low pressure and a cleaning solution to kill the algae and moss without touching the shingle surface, which is the method most roofing manufacturers actually require for warranty coverage.

How long does a typical pressure washing job take?

A driveway usually takes 45 minutes to 90 minutes depending on size and staining. A full house wash runs 1.5 to 3 hours. Roof soft washing takes 1 to 2 hours since the solution needs a short dwell time to work. We'll give you a time estimate when we quote the job so you can plan your day around it.
